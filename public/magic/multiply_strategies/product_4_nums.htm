Product of 4 Consecutive Integers Plus 1:

   A.  From algebra we know:

n x (n+1) x (n+2) x (n+3) + 1 = [n x (n+3) + 1]2

   B.  Using numbers instead of variables we get:

1.  Multiply the first and last of the consecutive numbers together.

2.  Add 1 to step 1.

3.  Square the result of step 2.

Ex [1]  5 x 6 x 7 x 8 + 1=_________.

a)  5 x 8 = 40.

b)  (40 + 1)2 = 1681.

c)  The answer is 1681.

Ex [2]  9 x 10 x 11 x 12 + 1 =_________.

a)  9 x 12 = 108.

b)  (108 + 1)2 = 11881.  See Multiplying Numbers Greater Than 100.

c)  The answer is 11881.
